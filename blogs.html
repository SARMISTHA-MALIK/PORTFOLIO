Adiyogi

Adiyogi is the first yogi and the originator of yoga.
Shiva is not only a Hindu god, but also the first yogi and the first guru.
Location : Isha Foundation in Chikkaballapur, Bangalore

The Ultimate Statue of Lord Shiva

Adiyogi is a 112-foot-tall bust of Lord Shiva located at the Isha Yoga Center in Chikkaballapur, Karnataka, India. It is the largest bust sculpture in the world. The statue was designed by Sadhguru Jaggi Vasudev, the founder of Isha Foundation.

The Adiyogi statue is based on a depiction of Lord Shiva as the first yogi. He is shown in a state of deep meditation, with his eyes closed and his hands in a gesture of yoga mudra. The statue is made of steel and concrete and is painted in a matte silver finish.

A Road Trip From Bangalore to Coorg

Coorg, also known as Kodagu, is a beautiful hill station located in the Western Ghats of India. It is a popular tourist destination, known for its lush green forests, coffee plantations, and waterfalls. Coorg is also a great place to go trekking, camping, and birdwatching.

Kotilingeswar

Kotilingeshwara Temple (Kannada: ಕೋಟಿಲಿಂಗೇಶ್ವರ)
A Hindu temple in the village of Kammasandra in Kolar district, Karnataka, India.
The presiding deity of the temple is Shiva.
The temple has one of the largest Shivalingams in the world.

The Biggest Shivalinga in Asia

‘Koti’ in Kannada means a crore and Kotilingeshwara is 1 crore Shivalingas.
The temple with the tallest Shivalinga in the world is installed here along with 90+ lakhs of other Shivalingas of different sizes.
The 33 mts high Shivalinga and 11 mts high Lord Nandi, the Bull are the main attractions of the temple. The temple is open for donations of Shivalingas of various sizes which can be installed with the name of the donor embedded on it.

Tk Falls

Thottikallu Falls, also known as TK Falls or SwarnaMukhi Falls, is a small waterfall near Bangalore.
It's about 20 km south of the city.

A Great Place to Relax

Thottikallu Falls, also known as TK Falls or Swarna Mukhi Falls, is a hidden gem near Bangalore that offers a refreshing escape from the city's hustle and bustle. This seasonal waterfall is surrounded by lush greenery and rocky terrain.

The waterfall is open from 6 AM to 6 PM and there is no entry fee. However, visitors need to park their vehicles at a distance and walk or trek for about 2 km to reach the waterfall. The trek to the waterfall is moderately difficult and involves crossing a stream and climbing a steep and slippery slope. The view from the top of the cliff is stunning.

Chaulabhaja

It is a hidden waterfall of about 100 feet.
Chaulabhija is a hidden gem of Odisha. ( 40 km from Baripada town)
Location : Similipal Forest, Mayurbhanj, Odisha

The Dangerous Trekking Ever

Chaulabhaja is a hidden gem of Odisha.

To reach the waterfall, one has to cross dense forests, steep hills, rocky terrains, and narrow streams. The trek is not for the faint-hearted, as it involves walking for about 6 hours, climbing slippery rocks, and crossing deep gorges. But the reward is worth the effort, as the sight of the majestic waterfall and the sound of its roaring water will leave you spellbound.
